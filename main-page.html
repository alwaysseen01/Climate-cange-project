Why temperature changing is dangerous?



- See these animals and plant in the pictures? What if they die out in 30 years?

Unfortunately, human activity can lead to irreparable consequences in the development of flora and fauna on the Earth. You can find more information by this link

In 2002, biologist E. O. Wilson calculated that if the current rate of anthropogenic destruction of the biosphere continues, half of all plant and animal species on Earth will disappear within 100 years. According to a 2003 survey conducted at 14 biodiversity research centres, 15-37% of terrestrial species are "dead to extinction" by 2050 due to climate change. The ecologically rich regions most threatened by losses are in southern Africa and the Caribbean.

Can climate change affect the social part of the whole world?

The impact of climate change on human society due to warming or changes in precipitation patterns, or both, has been found worldwide. But the future social impacts of climate change will be uneven. Risks are expected to rise as global warming increases. All regions are at risk of negative impact.

In 2014, a meta-analysis was conducted, according to which, for every 1 ° C increase in temperature, the level of violence increases by 20%, including fights, violent crimes, riots or wars.

A 2015 estimate based on the IPCC A1B emissions scenario showed that additional greenhouse gases released from permafrost would cost the global economy $43 trillion.

The yield of agricultural crops in middle and high latitudes with an increase in local temperatures by 1–3 ° C will slightly increase, but further warming will lead to its decrease. In low latitudes (especially in dry regions and in the tropics), agriculture is highly vulnerable. Even a slight increase in local temperatures (by 1 - 2 ° C) will increase the danger of starvation. Globally, the potential for agricultural production rises as local average temperatures rise to 1-3°C, falling as temperatures rise further.

As noted in a 2020 study, if global warming continues at the same pace, up to 3.5 billion people could live in uninhabitable weather zones in half a century. The total area of ​​the Earth, where the average annual temperature exceeds 29 degrees (as in the Sahara desert), will increase from 0.8% to 19% of the land. An increase in the average annual temperature on Earth for every additional degree can leave about a billion people without habitable living conditions.

Human activity on Earth exposes himself to risks and increases the rate of rising water levels, the danger and chance of forest fires around the world. Natural disasters take not only people's lives, but also economic prosperity, because they entail huge material damage (destruction of infrastructure, communications, etc.)

There is a huge amount of research on this topic. You can freely read them on the UN website or here.

Can global warming affect human health in the long run?

Answer is immensely simple - YES

WHO claims that climate change affects social and environmental health factors such as clean air, safe drinking water, adequate food and safe shelter.
In general, the impact on public health will be more negative than positive. Extreme weather will cause injury and death, crop failures threaten malnutrition.

In some regions, there will be a transition from cold to heat mortality. In 2018, the US Centers for Disease Control and Prevention conducted a study that linked fever and an increase in the number of suicides. The paper suggests that hot days increase the number of suicides and could cause more than 26,000 suicides in the US by 2050.
