There're few steps how we can stop it

Just use the social transport more!



The choice of means of transport in the city has a significant impact on the level of greenhouse gas emissions. You can give up your personal car in favor of walking, cycling or public transport.

Dr. Debra Roberts, one of the co-chairs of the IPCC, says: "If you don't have public transport, you as a voter can step in and vote for politicians who are in favor of its development."

If you have no choice but to use your car, opt for electric cars, and on long journeys take the train rather than the plane. Skip the next business trip and replace it with a video conference.

Consume energy wisely!



Dry your laundry on a line, not in an electric dryer. Set the heating thermostat to a lower switch-on temperature. Install thermal insulation in the attic and under the roof to reduce heat loss in the winter.

Unplug electrical appliances that you do not use. Consider installing energy efficient systems such as a solar water heater.

These measures may seem trivial, but they can actually save a lot of energy.

Cut down on meat!


The production of meat entails significantly higher greenhouse gas emissions than the production of chicken, vegetables, fruits and grains.






But you personally can do a lot. Reduce your meat intake in favor of vegetables and fruits. If this is difficult to achieve, try not eating meat once a week.

Recycle everything!

We are constantly told about the importance of collecting household waste. But the transportation and processing of such waste is accompanied by the release of a large amount of CO2 into the atmosphere. Recycling leads to a reduction in energy consumption, but it would be even better to reuse products or reduce consumption.

The same applies to water consumption. "We have to save and regenerate water while getting more people involved in rainwater harvesting," says Aromar Revi.

Inform and educate!

Knowledge about climate change and what it brings with it needs to be spread as widely as possible. Create community groups to promote sustainable farming practices at the local level.

Create interest groups for the purpose of joint operation of garden equipment.

"All these measures, if they are used by billions of people every day, will ensure sustainable development and hardly affect the standard of living of the population," Aromar Revi said.
